Interactive Art

2019

The Look of Sound is an interactive art piece that plays with the conventions of light and sound, showing how it interacts and coexists together.

Music production culture is shifting.

What used to look like a professional in a big expensive studio, can now be a teenager in their bedroom equipped with a laptop and a microphone. It’s less about perfection, and more about self expression.

Challenge

Since our end of first year project, “The Look Of Sound” we had the idea to extend the functionality of the launchpad we created, to make it more like a looper that would allow you to build your own songs by pressing buttons that would trigger loops, separated into rows of drums, bass, chords, melodies, and fx.

Instead of spending a lot of time on making a physical prototype, knowing it wasn’t where I wanted the project to end, I prototyped it’s functionality on Processing to learn how to code the looping and tempo elements that I knew I would need later on in the process.

Our Goals

To make music production more accessible for children and young teens. By reducing the learning curve and providing a tool that is intuitive yet affordable.

Explore the concept of how is "Music is a Playground for Sound."

Innovate. Experiment. Be playful. Make something WACK.

Approach

I decided to use this project for my entrepreneurship and innovation paper to help learn and explore how this project can be extended beyond just the showcase, and find a place in the world that can marketed as a product that is actually of use for an intended target audience.

Throughout the progression in this paper, I realised what I truly wanted to make, and who I wanted to make it for. I think all this information was subconsciously all there when I decided I wanted to create instruments, and this paper brought it to my conscious thoughts.

For example, I knew I wanted to make this ‘looping launchpad’ last year when I was showing my younger cousin Josh, aged 8, how to use my Akai MPK midi keyboard. I had already assigned sounds to each pad, so all he had to do was press them and play around with. He enjoyed this so much, that he asked me if I could get him one for his birthday. Although I could go out and buy him one, I knew this wouldn’t be of any use to him because he didn’t have access to a laptop or a digital audio workstation.

This sparked my idea to create something that would allow him to create music without needing any of that.
